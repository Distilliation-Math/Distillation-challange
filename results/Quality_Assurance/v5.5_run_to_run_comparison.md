## Key Findings

1. **Llama is essentially deterministic** — only 2/669 problems flipped across runs. reasoning_effort="none" and the default temperature=0 in official params make it fully reproducible.

2. **Gemma is mostly stable** — 6% mismatch rate. Results are reproducible within ~1% accuracy. The most reliable model for benchmarking cheatsheet improvements.

3. **GPT-OSS-120b is highly stochastic** — 26% of answers flip between runs (temperature=1.0 required by reasoning models). On hard2 it dropped 6% accuracy purely from random variance, with FP jumping from 14 to 25.

4. **GPT's variance is symmetric on large datasets** — on hard1 and hard3, the flips canceled out perfectly (6 vs 6, 55 vs 55). Hard2's asymmetry (32 regressions vs 20 improvements) is within expected noise.

5. **Aggregate scores are stable despite per-problem noise** — gemma's macro F1 moved only ±0.01. GPT's hard2 was the outlier (-0.057 macro F1).

6. **Implications for cheatsheet optimization:** A +2% improvement on GPT may be within noise. For reliable signal, prioritize gemma results (low variance) and require consistent gains across multiple datasets. GPT improvements should be validated across at least 2 runs.
