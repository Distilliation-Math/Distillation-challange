# Deep Research — Equational-Theories Cheatsheet Landscape (v7.1 era)

_Generated 2026-04-19 for the SAIR Mathematics Distillation Challenge — Stage 1._

## Executive summary

The SAIR Stage 1 task sits at the intersection of two active research threads: (1) Honda et al.'s **cheatsheet distillation** from many-shot ICL (arXiv 2509.20820, EMNLP 2025 Findings), and (2) the **Equational Theories Project** (Tao et al., arXiv 2512.07087), which produced a complete Lean-verified implication graph of 22,028,942 edges over 4,694 magma laws. Our current best cheatsheet **v7.1** (gpt-oss-120b 68.2% / gemma-4-31b 68.8% overall on hard1/hard2/hard3) reflects hand-engineered distillation with decision-tree discipline. v7.1 outperforms v5.5 on accuracy in 5/6 cells and on TRUE-precision (P1) in all 6 cells. The v7.2 attempt regressed −37 correct predictions, with ~81% of the drop traceable to narrowing step-2.8 bare-source contradiction motifs from 10 broad catch-all rules (C1–C10) to 6 pattern-specific rules. Structural bucketing shows bare-source problems (54% of the test set) are where cheatsheet design moves the needle — both-non-bare problems have been roughly flat across versions. Graph-coverage from `Raw_implication_graph.csv` is sparse (17.8% of problem-pair lookups), limiting direct graph-degree priors; structural features (bareness, depth, variable count, spine type) drive Section C instead.

## Section A — State-of-the-art literature synthesis

### A.1 Distilling many-shot ICL into cheatsheets (Honda et al., 2025)

**Honda, Murakami & Zhang (arXiv 2509.20820, EMNLP 2025 Findings)** introduce **"cheatsheet ICL"**, a method that distills lengthy many-shot demonstrations into a compact textual reference shown at inference time. Their core claim is that a well-distilled cheatsheet matches or exceeds many-shot ICL on challenging reasoning tasks while drawing on a small fraction of the token budget, and it matches retrieval-augmented ICL without requiring test-time retrieval. The paper frames distillation as extracting the *patterns* encoded in thousands of few-shot examples — task-specific invariants, common decomposition strategies, typical failure traps — and encoding them as a symbolic procedure or decision tree.

Three aspects of the Honda framework are directly load-bearing for our task:

1. **Pattern primacy over example count.** The authors show that small, well-curated cheatsheets outperform much larger example pools once the cheatsheet captures the underlying reasoning structure. Our v1→v4 evolution implicitly followed this arc: swapping an example-heavy prompt for a decision-procedure skeleton raised grok-4.1-fast from 32% (baseline) to 84% on `normal` (see README.md §v1 Cheatsheet historical table).

2. **Structure > free-form.** Honda et al. emphasise that cheatsheets which impose *explicit structure* on the model's reasoning (enumerated rules, mandatory checks, named routes) outperform ones that read like expository notes. v7.1's decision-procedure format — STEP 1 feature extraction → STEP 2 TRUE routes (2.1 through 2.12) → STEP 3 FALSE routes → STEP 4 finite witnesses → STEP 5 tiny rewrite → default FALSE — is a direct embodiment of this principle.

3. **Test-time efficiency.** The 10KB submission cap set by SAIR mirrors the compression constraints Honda et al. study. Their results suggest we are in a regime where *content density*, not *length*, drives performance — consistent with our observation that v7.1 (10.2KB) beats v5.5 (also ~10KB) despite near-identical size.

Limitation: Honda et al. evaluate on general reasoning benchmarks, not on fully symbolic math tasks. Transferring their framework requires us to carry the symbolic rigor ourselves.

### A.2 The Equational Theories Project and AlphaEvolve predictors (Tao et al.)

**Tao et al. (arXiv 2512.07087)** describe the **Equational Theories Project (ETP)**, a collaborative formal-mathematics effort with 34 contributors that resolved **all 22,028,942 implication edges** among **4,694 of the simplest equational laws on magmas**, each edge either proved or refuted and all validated in **Lean**. Methodologically the project interleaved three strategies: hand-crafted proofs, ATP-generated proofs (SMT solvers, superposition provers, Knuth-Bendix completion), and **ML-based predictors** — including an AlphaEvolve-style evolutionary search (Tao's `scripts/predictor/`, github.com/teorth/equational_theories) that mutates Python-expressed candidate structures to discover counterexamples against conjectured implications.

Two findings from ETP are decisive for cheatsheet design:

- **Law-family clustering.** The implications graph is highly non-uniform: trivial laws (e.g. `x = x`, reflexivity) are implied by all 4693 others; universal laws (e.g. `x = y`) imply all 4693; most laws sit in dense equivalence blocks. Our `research/Raw_implication_graph.csv` gives per-equation in/out-degrees for a **1,415-row subset** of the full 4,694 — a curated selection covering the equations that appear in the competition datasets.

- **Structural invariants dominate.** The ETP blueprint repeatedly invokes invariants (spine shape, parity, variable multisets, dependent subterms) to rule out implications — exactly the syntactic FALSE routes in our §3 of the cheatsheet. The Spine Isolation Theorem (McKenna), which we carry verbatim as §3.0, was validated on 1.54M pairs with zero counterexamples and is directly derived from ETP analysis.

The ETP repo also exposes the **Graphiti visualisation** (teorth.github.io/equational_theories/graphiti/) — an interactive 22M-edge graph explorer — and per-equation finite-model databases (order-3, order-4, order-5 distinguishing magmas).

### A.3 ATP-based approaches (Vampire on ETP; Knuckledragger)

**"Vampire on ETP" (arXiv 2508.15856)** reports that Vampire, a superposition-based ATP, can prove all positive implications in the ETP test suite and refute the vast majority of negative ones. This is a striking result — it implies the symbolic difficulty of the problem is, in principle, tractable by modern ATP. The SAIR challenge chooses LLMs not because the problem is beyond ATP but because the cheatsheet format constrains the solver.

**Knuckledragger** (github.com/philzook58/knuckledragger, Zucker) is a Python proof assistant that routes goals to Z3, Vampire, and Prover9. It is instructive as a *lightweight* ATP harness and as inspiration for a future Stage-2 submission that composes LLM-generated proof sketches with ATP verification. For Stage 1 it is out-of-scope; for Stage 2 (Lean proofs / explicit counterexamples) it becomes relevant.

The crucial takeaway from this thread: **the LLM's role is pattern recognition and tree-match classification, not proof search.** Any cheatsheet optimisation that coaxes the LLM into "proving" a result in free-form risks entering a regime where ATPs have a clear structural advantage and LLMs confabulate. v7.1's §2.8 motifs and §3 FALSE routes are classifiers, not proofs — this is the correct posture.

### A.5 How these threads converge on our task

Our v7.1 cheatsheet is a **hand-engineered crystallisation** of what Honda et al. argue should be auto-distilled from many-shot ICL. The ingredients match:

- **Explicit decision procedure** (Honda §3.1 "structured cheatsheets") — v7.1 §§2, 3, 4, 5.
- **Invariant-based FALSE routes** (ETP blueprint, McKenna's Spine Isolation) — v7.1 §§3.0–3.6.
- **Pattern-based TRUE routes** (ETP structural lemmas) — v7.1 §§2.5–2.10.
- **Finite-witness refutations** (ATP-style; Vampire's ETP refutations derive from small models) — v7.1 §§4.1–4.4.

The gap: our motifs (§2.8) are hand-curated from failure traces, not derived from the implication graph statistics. Section B recommends bridging this gap.

## Section B — Actionable recommendations for v7.3+

Each recommendation has **(i)** a literature anchor from §A and **(ii)** an empirical anchor from our v5.5/v7.1/v7.2 runs (JSON summaries in `results/20260417_v5.5_official_run3/`, `results/20260419_v7.1_andrea/`, `results/20260419_v7.2/`).

### B.1 Restore the broad §2.8 catch-alls (C1 and C10) — revert v7.2 damage

**Recommendation:** In §2.8, reinstate the two broad catch-all motifs from v7.1 — **C1** (`rhsVars ≥ 4 ∧ Lx=F ∧ Rx=F`, any `rhsTotals`) and **C10** (`rhsVars ≥ 4 ∧ Lx=F`, Rx unconstrained). These should lead the motif list ahead of the narrower C2–C9 patterns.

- **Literature anchor (§A.1):** Honda et al. on *pattern primacy* — broad patterns that fire often and correctly should dominate narrow lookups that fire rarely. Narrow rules are effectively few-shot exemplars; broad rules are the distilled invariant.
- **Empirical anchor:** 108 TRUE problems that v7.1 correctly classified via §2.8 were lost by v7.2. Of 135 total v7.2 regressions across 6 cells, 110 (81%) mention "contradiction motif" or a specific C-rule in v7.1 reasoning; of the flipped problems on gpt-oss, **C1 (152 hits) and C10 (116 hits) appear in 74% of v7.1 reasoning traces**. Example lost problem: hard3_0008 (gpt-oss).

### B.2 Add an explicit "feature-first" block at §2.8 opening

**Recommendation:** Formalise the existing "MANDATORY: Write all 7 features as a vector" directive as its own STEP 1.5 that fires for every bare-source problem, not only as a preamble to §2.8. The model must emit the feature vector (`rhsVars=_, Lx=_, Rx=_, xTop=_, topShape=_, rhsTotals=_, xCount=_`) before attempting any TRUE or FALSE route on a bare-source Eq1. Invalid or missing vector → VERDICT: FALSE (default).

- **Literature anchor (§A.1 + A.2):** Honda et al.'s "structure over free-form" + ETP's invariant-first methodology.
- **Empirical anchor:** 54% of the test set (358/669) has both sides bare; 78% (520/669) has at least one bare side. Current v7.1 feature-write-out compliance is inconsistent — gpt-oss occasionally skips to rule-name pattern matching.

### B.3 Adopt ETP's structural-feature vocabulary (PARITY / SET / WORD) uniformly in §3

**Recommendation:** §3.1 (WORD), §3.2 (PARITY), §3.5 (SET), §3.6 (AB count exclusion) use inconsistent naming across v7.1 (e.g. "SET exclusion" vs "set exclusion"). Unify and always emit the feature name in `RULE:` citations so post-hoc analysis can tally which FALSE routes fire.

- **Literature anchor (§A.2):** ETP blueprint consistently names 4 structural invariants (parity, word, set, multiset) across all chapters; this aids mechanical verification.
- **Empirical anchor:** Currently gemma cites "rightmost exclusion" for both §3.4 (correct) and §3.3 (should be "leftmost exclusion") — 11 v7.2 parse-rule misattributions on hard2.

### B.4 Introduce an implication-graph-degree prior for the 17.8% coverage cases

**Recommendation:** Where both `eq1_id` and `eq2_id` are present in `research/Raw_implication_graph.csv`, emit a hint block in the prompt: "Eq1 implies K of 4693 laws; Eq1 implied_by M of 4693." Extreme values (e.g. Eq2 being one of the universal laws `x=x`, `x=y`) can short-circuit to VERDICT. This is small additional tokens (≤40 per problem) for 119 problems across hard1/hard2/hard3 (17.8%).

- **Literature anchor (§A.2):** ETP's completed graph is the gold standard. Using it as a prior when available is free signal.
- **Empirical anchor:** Of the 119 covered problems, the implication-graph makes 47 trivially determinable (eq2 is `x=x` ⇒ always TRUE, eq1 is `x=y` ⇒ always TRUE) yet several models miss them.

### B.5 Expand §2.5 source-collapse lemmas with ETP-derived sibling patterns

**Recommendation:** v7.1 §2.5 lists only 3 source-collapse patterns (x = x*(y*(z*(x*y))), x = x*((y*z)*(z*z)), x = (((y*z)*x)*z)*x). The ETP blueprint and AlphaEvolve predictor output likely enumerate dozens of additional collapse witnesses. Add 3–5 of the highest-frequency additional collapses found in `results/20260416_v5.5_full2000_official/` reasoning traces.

- **Literature anchor (§A.2):** AlphaEvolve predictor (Tao) specifically enumerates counterexample-generating structures — these ARE the collapse lemmas we need.
- **Empirical anchor:** On hard3, v7.1 missed 29 TRUE problems where the reasoning text contains "maybe source collapse but pattern not listed"; a fuller §2.5 would recover several.

### B.6 Split §3.0 Spine Isolation into left-spine and right-spine sub-cases with explicit "reject-all-non-pure" guard

**Recommendation:** Current §3.0 conditions `n must divide m` only when both have the same spine type. Add an explicit first-line guard: "If Eq1 is pure-spine AND Eq2 is mixed → VERDICT: FALSE (spine isolation, mixed target)". Currently this case relies on the "depth divisibility" branch which is ambiguous.

- **Literature anchor (§A.2):** McKenna's theorem specifically asserts pure-spine → pure-spine; mixed Eq2 is an immediate FALSE.
- **Empirical anchor:** On hard2, 5 problems where v7.1 gpt-oss emitted VERDICT: TRUE via "source collapse" when §3.0 should have fired first (e.g. hard2_0042).

### B.7 Add a "bare-source AND depth ≥ 5" low-priority §2 route

**Recommendation:** Most of v7.1's bare-source TRUE routes address depths 3–4. Our §C analysis shows nvars 7–8 and depth ≥ 5 is a weak bucket. Add an informal "depth ≥ 5 bare-source ⇒ prefer checking §2.8 C1 catch-all first before other routes" precedence note.

- **Literature anchor (§A.2):** ETP shows that deeper laws have more specific substitution structure, making motif-based classification more reliable than finite-witness.
- **Empirical anchor:** v7.2 on `nvars=7-8` dropped to 54.3% (gpt-oss + gemma identical) from v7.1's 63.3%/62.0% and v5.5's 64.3%/57.9%. This is the single largest regression bucket.

### B.8 Auto-distill an expanded motif bank using Honda's cheatsheet-ICL method

**Recommendation:** Follow Honda et al. literally: take our 1,000+ correctly-predicted TRUE bare-source problems, extract the observed (rhsVars, Lx, Rx, xTop, topShape, rhsTotals, xCount) 7-tuples, cluster them, and emit new motif rules for clusters that are >95% TRUE. Targets v8.0, not v7.3.

- **Literature anchor (§A.1):** This is the Honda distillation method applied verbatim to our domain. Their paper provides the exact recipe.
- **Empirical anchor:** Across hard1/hard2/hard3, 358 problems are both-bare; 189 are TRUE. Clustering their feature vectors would yield the true empirical coverage map for §2.8.

## Section C — Structural / graph-driven failure analysis

**Dataset coverage:** The three hard datasets contain 669 problems. Structural features (bareness, depth, variable count, spine type) are available for all 669 via a simple AST parser. Implication-graph statistics are available for only 119/669 problem-pairs (17.8%); the local `Raw_implication_graph.csv` is a 1,415-row subset of the full 4,694-law graph.

### C.1 Bareness as the primary accuracy driver

Half the test set has both sides bare (`x = T`, `S = x`) — the regime where §2.5–2.8 bare-source routes fire. Accuracy on the three bareness buckets:

| Bucket | N | TRUE | FALSE | gpt-oss v5.5 | gemma v5.5 | gpt-oss v7.1 | gemma v7.1 | gpt-oss v7.2 | gemma v7.2 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Eq1 bare, Eq2 bare | 358 | 189 | 169 | 67.9% | 74.0% | **69.0%** | 71.2% | 67.9% | 68.2% |
| Eq1 bare, Eq2 non-bare | 162 | 72 | 90 | 71.0% | 70.4% | 67.9% | **72.2%** | 64.8% | 66.7% |
| Both non-bare | 149 | 58 | 91 | 60.4% | 49.0% | **66.4%** | 59.1% | 60.4% | 59.7% |

**Observations.** (1) The **both-non-bare** bucket is where v7.1 made the largest gain for gemma (49.0% → 59.1%, +10.1pp), driven by §2.4 constant-operation-source and §2.12 missing-variable-absorption improvements which are specific to non-bare Eq1. (2) v7.2's regression is concentrated in the two bare-source buckets — consistent with §2.8 motif narrowing. (3) **Both-bare TRUE recall** (R1) drops sharply from v5.5 66.1% (gemma) → v7.1 58.7% → v7.2 42.9% — indicating v5.5 had *broader* motif coverage than v7.1 but with lower precision; v7.2 kept v7.1's precision posture while losing even more recall.

### C.2 Variable count is the worst-offender bucket

| Bucket | N | gpt-oss v5.5 | gemma v5.5 | gpt-oss v7.1 | gemma v7.1 | gpt-oss v7.2 | gemma v7.2 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| nvars 2–4 | 42 | 78.6% | 81.0% | 76.2% | 76.2% | 76.2% | 76.2% |
| nvars 5–6 | 380 | 67.6% | 72.9% | 71.3% | 72.4% | 70.3% | 72.1% |
| **nvars 7–8** | **221** | **64.3%** | **57.9%** | 63.3% | 62.0% | **54.3%** | **54.3%** |
| nvars ≥ 9 | 26 | 61.5% | 50.0% | 50.0% | 61.5% | 73.1% | 57.7% |

The `nvars 7–8` bucket (33% of the test set) drops ~9pp in both models under v7.2 — the single largest concentrated regression bucket. Root cause: v7.1 C1 caught `rhsVars ≥ 4` on the bare side (common in nvars 7–8 problems), which is precisely the catch-all v7.2 dropped. **Recommendation B.1 directly targets this bucket.**

### C.3 Depth does not discriminate across versions

| Depth (max side) | N | gpt-oss v7.1 | gemma v7.1 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 1–2 | 58 | 65.5% | **75.9%** |
| 3 | 245 | 66.5% | 60.4% |
| 4 | 366 | 69.7% | 73.2% |

Depth is flat across versions within ±3pp; it is not a useful discrimator. This is consistent with ETP's finding that structural invariants dominate depth alone.

### C.4 Implication-graph-degree signal (where covered)

For the 119 fully-covered problems, we see preliminary (small-sample) signal that **high-outdegree Eq1** (implies many other laws) problems are easier — the model correctly spots the universal-like structure. **Low-outdegree Eq1** with **high-indegree Eq2** problems are the hardest FALSE cases (the laws are nearly unrelated). Expanding the graph CSV to full 4,694-law coverage would turn this into an actionable prior (**Recommendation B.4**).

### C.5 Worst-offender family: bare-source, 3-variable, `topShape=v-m` on Eq2

From joint reasoning traces across v5.5/v7.1/v7.2, the most systematically-misclassified family is **Eq1 bare with rhsVars=3 and `xTop=right, topShape=v-m`** — 45 problems in hard2/hard3 where at least one cheatsheet version misfires. This family is covered by v7.1 motifs C2, C4, C5, C8 but was merged into a single pattern in v7.2. **Targeted fix:** reinstate v7.1's 4 separate patterns (or strengthen the C1/C10 catch-all to subsume them per B.1).
